Well, there's quite a few options I can think of. I'd go with a Reaper at the moment, personally. Displacing item? The one weapon in ME 3 based on the Reaper's molten iron stream thing. Token? The Husk-making spike, reworked to be a practical stabbing implement and set to make the Husks loyal to the stabber rather than hooked up to a Reaper-centred network. But it'd need being turned on by the Reaper-displaced.

Why? Well, given time to restructure the internals, telekinesis is doable by way of biotics, FTL is just a thing they can do, Husks can be used for access to magic and telepresence and a lot of Eezo screwing is available. Of note is that Eezo decays over the course of centuries of use, so the whole "1,000 years of lockdown" thing leads to a crippled Reaper who needs an Eezo restock to get back up and running. Fortunately, MLP Magic can be reasonably assumed to be capable of reality-screwing to make Eezo from scratch. Or at least making additional Eezo with access to existing Eezo.

Yes, I'd make new Reapers. But as reasonably-sized super-husks rather than space-cuttlefish. And by "reasonably sized," I mean the level of difference in size between Celestia and the average pony. I'd keep to the "one of each species" rule, counting all the ponies as one species. I'd also keep their mentalities close to the species of origin, rather than the complete overhaul that Reapers typically do. So they'd be immortal cybernetic things able to learn how to be part of the culture their species has, possibly ending up as immortal rulers that get torn up one by one by the ponies with obsessive amounts of protagonist-centred morality. Because apparently the giant army wrecking thing that defiles the laws of nature can't be a friendly eldritch abomination trying to drag the world up to its level without the nastiness of the world getting there alone...
